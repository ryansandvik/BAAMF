# BAAMF App — Context Document

## Overview

BAAMF is a private book club with 12 fixed members. Each member hosts one meeting per year on a rotating schedule set at the start of the calendar year. The app manages the full lifecycle of each month: submission, voting, scoring, and history.

---

## User Roles

**Member** — standard access. Can submit books, vote, score, and veto.

**Administrator** — superuser. Has all Member privileges plus all Host privileges (can set submission mode, open/close voting rounds, enter scores on behalf of all members, and force host schedule changes). Assigned manually in Firebase (or equivalent auth system).

**Host** — the member whose month it is. Can set submission mode, post event info, and open/close voting rounds. Every member is a host once per year by default.

---

## Host Schedule

- Set once at the start of the calendar year, one member per month
- Any member can request a swap with another member at any time during the year
- Both parties should consent to a swap, but an Administrator can force one
- A member can end up hosting more than once in a year (e.g. if a member needs to give up their month, they can pass it to a willing member, who then hosts twice)

---

## Monthly Lifecycle

### 1. Host Setup (~6 weeks before the meeting)
The host selects a submission mode and optionally posts event info (date, location, etc).

**Submission Modes:**
- **Open** — any member may submit one book
- **Theme** — host defines a theme; any member may submit one book fitting that theme
- **Host Selection (Pick-4)** — host submits exactly 4 books; no other members may submit

Submission is optional in Open and Theme modes — members only submit if they're passionate about a book.

---

### 2. Submissions
- Members submit a book by searching/entering title + author
- App auto-fetches from Goodreads: description, rating, page count
- Submitter can override the description with their own pitch to the club
- Each member may submit at most 1 book (except host in Pick-4, who submits exactly 4)

---

### 3. Vetoes (before voting begins)

**Type 1 — Already Read:**
- Any member can veto a book they've already read
- Book is immediately removed from the pool
- No charge or cooldown consumed

**Type 2 — Don't Want to Read:**
- Each member has **2 charges**, each with a **12-month cooldown per use**
- If at least **25% of voting members** also cast this veto on the same book, the book stays but starts with **-2 votes** (requires 4 votes in round 1 to reach a net total of 2)
- If the 25% threshold is not met, the book stays with no penalty

---

### 4. Voting — Round 1
- Happens **in-person at the meeting**, synchronously
- **Anonymous**
- Each member gets **2 votes** to distribute across eligible books
- Host (or Administrator) manually advances to Round 2

---

### 5. Voting — Round 2
- Top 2 books from Round 1 advance; top 3 advance only in case of a tie for 2nd place
- Always at least 2 books, even if one book dominated Round 1
- **Anonymous**
- Each member gets **1 vote**
- Winning book is selected for the club to read

---

### 6. The Meeting & Scoring
- Scoring opens at the meeting start time
- Members discuss the book, then enter scores individually — or an Administrator can enter all scores on behalf of the group
- Scores are on a **1–7 scale**; half-points allowed (e.g. 3.5), though discouraged
- Members can update their score any time until the host or Administrator closes scoring
- All individual scores are visible to all members (not anonymous)

---

## Historical Records
All past books are visible with: group average score, each member's individual score, Goodreads data, and submission info.

---

## Tech Notes
- **Platform:** iOS only
- **Auth:** Firebase (or similar); Administrator role assigned manually
- **Goodreads integration:** auto-fetch on book submission (description, rating, page count)
- **Voting method:** should be easy to swap/configure
